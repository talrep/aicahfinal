Hymeria defines itself as an AI-augmented strategy consulting firm: our engagements combine the expert judgment of our consultants with the contribution of artificial-intelligence tools (hereinafter "AI"). This section describes our approach and the associated safeguards, in the spirit of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 of June 13, 2024 laying down harmonized rules on artificial intelligence (the "AI Act") and the GDPR.

7.1 Nature of the systems used

As part of its services and, where applicable, on the site, Hymeria may use various AI systems, including: (i) generative AI models to produce syntheses, analyses, visuals or preliminary proposals; (ii) data-analysis tools for exploration, classification and modeling; (iii) conversational assistants for documentary research. These tools are deployed as third-party components or developed in-house.

7.2 Principle of human oversight

Any output from an AI system in the context of Hymeria's engagements is subject to human review by a qualified consultant who checks its relevance, consistency, the truthfulness of the sources used and the alignment with the scope of the engagement. The final judgment, recommendations and binding deliverables of Hymeria fall under the responsibility of its consultants and partners, not the AI itself.

7.3 No fully automated individual decisions

Hymeria does not make any decision producing legal effects on data subjects, or significantly affecting them in a similar way, based solely on automated processing, within the meaning of Article 22 of the GDPR. All significant decisions involve substantial human intervention.

7.4 Confidentiality and no training

Personal data and confidential client information are not used to train third-party AI models for commercial purposes. When Hymeria uses external models, it prioritizes contractual offerings excluding training on submitted content ("no-training clauses") and governs these processings through subprocessor agreements compliant with Article 28 of the GDPR. Internal instructions specify the types of data that may be shared with each tool.

7.5 Fairness, transparency and quality

Hymeria informs its clients, within each engagement, of the use of AI tools and, where relevant, of the associated limitations. Deliverables likely to contain AI-generated or AI-assisted elements are identifiable internally and undergo reinforced quality control. Hymeria endeavors to assess the risk of bias and to limit its effects through source diversification, methodological challenge and validation by human expertise.

7.6 Inherent limits of AI systems

The user and client are informed that, despite the precautions implemented, AI systems may produce inaccurate, incomplete or outdated results ("hallucinations"), reflect biases stemming from their training data, or evolve in their performance. No content produced with AI assistance on this site shall, in the absence of express written validation by Hymeria within a contractual engagement, constitute legal, tax, financial, medical or professional advice binding on Hymeria.
